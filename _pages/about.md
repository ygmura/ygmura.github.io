I am an MA graduate in Speech and Language Processing at [University of Konstanz](https://www.uni-konstanz.de/). My research primarily revolves around third language acquisition, typology, cross-linguistic influence and morphosyntax. I am drawn to the spaces where languages meet, overlap, and sometimes fail to align.

I am particularly interested in
* how the influence of previously acquired languages shapes the course of third or further language acquisition,
* how similar the structures must be to invite transfer,
* and how factors such as proficiency, language use, input, and social context modulate it.

I explore these questions using experimental methods and statistical analysis. 

I completed my BA in Linguistics at [Dokuz Eylül University](https://global.deu.edu.tr/). I later received my MSc in Speech and Language Processing from [University of Konstanz](https://www.uni-konstanz.de/). As part of my graduate studies, I spent a semester abroad at [Norwegian University of Science and Technology (NTNU)](https://www.ntnu.edu/). For more details, please see my CV.

Alongside my CV, the blog is where I think more freely. I write about my real-life experiences tied to linguistic theories.
